# Marketing Plan

## Positioning

Construction Change-Order & Permit OS is the operating layer between permit friction and financial execution for commercial GC teams. It helps project teams see which permit issues are turning into schedule and margin exposure, get pricing moving faster, and push cleaner owner-facing change-order packages through approval.

## Ideal customer profile

- US mid-market commercial general contractors
- 10 to 250 employees
- Multiple active projects with a mix of permit tracking, owner scope changes, and subcontractor pricing coordination

### Buyer

- COO
- VP Operations
- CFO
- Director of Project Controls

### Champion

- Project executive
- Project manager
- Project engineer

## Core pains to market against

1. Permit blockers live in one spreadsheet, pricing lives in email, and owner approvals live in PDF chains.
2. Scope changes are discovered too late, after crews have already absorbed margin pressure.
3. PMs cannot show leadership a clean view of pending permit risk, pending approvals, and at-risk dollars in one place.
4. Teams lose time rebuilding owner-facing change-order packages from scratch.
5. Operational knowledge is tribal and inconsistent from one project team to the next.

## Messaging pillars

### See risk earlier

- Turn permit delays and revision requests into visible cost and schedule exposure.
- Show which jobs are drifting before the monthly review meeting.

### Move pricing and approvals faster

- Link change events to RFQs, quotes, and change orders in one workflow.
- Reduce lag between field discovery, pricing, review, and owner submission.

### Standardize execution

- Give PMs, PEs, finance approvers, and executives the same project truth.
- Create auditability without adding another spreadsheet layer.

### Package work cleanly

- Export change-order packages and project snapshots without manual reassembly.
- Make internal and external reviews more repeatable.

## Category strategy

Do not market this as a generic construction ERP or PM platform. The wedge is narrower and stronger:

- permit register
- linked change management
- subcontractor pricing coordination
- approval routing
- owner package generation

## Competitive angle

- Against spreadsheets: single workflow, structured data, audit trail, and faster handoff between permit issues and change execution.
- Against broad construction suites: faster time to value for one painful cross-functional workflow.
- Against point permit products: better financial follow-through once the permit issue becomes margin and schedule exposure.

## Offer structure

### Pilot

- 4 to 8 weeks
- one workspace
- 1 to 3 active jobs
- onboarding plus white-glove workflow setup

### Post-pilot

- annual contract
- platform fee plus role-based seats
- optional premium add-ons for S3 retention, analytics, and custom integrations

## Go-to-market motion

### Phase 1

- founder-led outbound to GCs with active commercial projects
- message around permit blockers, unpriced scope drift, and delayed owner approvals
- demo with a realistic permit-to-change-order example, not a generic dashboard tour

### Phase 2

- publish operational content on:
  - permit blockers becoming margin leakage
  - how to run a cleaner change-order register
  - what project controls leaders should see weekly

### Phase 3

- partner with construction consultants, project controls advisors, and specialty operational coaches

## Sales proof to build

- one anonymized pilot case study
- one sample owner package
- one short demo video showing permit issue -> change event -> quote -> approval flow
- one before/after workflow comparison against spreadsheet-heavy teams

## Launch metrics

- active jobs per workspace
- time from project creation to first permit recorded
- time from change event creation to change-order submission
- percentage of change orders with routed approvals
- export rate for owner packages and project snapshots
- weekly active PM and PE users
