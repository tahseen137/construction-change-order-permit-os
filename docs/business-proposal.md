# Business Proposal

## Executive summary

Construction Change-Order & Permit OS is a workflow product for commercial GCs that connects permit blockers, scope drift, subcontractor pricing, owner approvals, and exportable change-order packages in one system. The goal is to reduce operational leakage between field issues and financial execution.

## Problem

Commercial GC teams frequently manage permit status, RFIs, pricing conversations, and change-order packages in disconnected spreadsheets, inboxes, and shared drives. That fragmentation leads to:

- delayed visibility into cost and schedule exposure
- inconsistent approval routing
- slower owner submissions
- weaker audit trails
- margin leakage from unpriced or poorly tracked scope changes

## Solution

The product provides:

- workspace and user management
- project registry
- permit register with blocker tracking
- linked change-event workflow
- RFQ and quote coordination
- change-order creation and approval routing
- owner-package export
- audit activity and operational dashboarding

## Target customer

- US mid-market commercial general contractors
- initial focus on office, industrial, retail, healthcare, and mixed-use jobs

## Buyer and champion

### Buyer

- COO
- VP Operations
- CFO
- Director of Project Controls

### Champion

- Project executive
- Project manager
- Project engineer

## Value proposition

- Reduce time from permit issue to priced and routed change order.
- Improve visibility into at-risk dollars and schedule slip.
- Standardize approvals and project controls without forcing a full ERP replacement.
- Provide cleaner owner-facing packages and better internal auditability.

## Pricing model

### Pilot

- fixed-fee paid pilot
- 1 workspace
- 1 to 3 projects
- onboarding and workflow configuration included

### Production contract

- annual contract
- platform fee plus role-based seats
- premium pricing for advanced storage, analytics, or integration needs

## Sales motion

- founder-led outbound into named GC accounts
- live workflow demo using a realistic permit blocker and change-order scenario
- short pilot with success criteria around time-to-package, workflow adoption, and visibility into risk

## Success metrics

- active projects per workspace
- permit-to-change-event conversion visibility
- average time from change event creation to change-order submission
- approval turnaround time
- export usage
- weekly active project users

## Expansion path

Once the core permit/change-order workflow is adopted, expansion can move into:

- municipality-specific filing helpers
- schedule system integrations
- ERP and accounting sync
- subcontractor collaboration portals
- mobile jobsite capture

## Why this wedge works

This product avoids competing as a generic construction platform. Instead, it focuses on one painful operational seam where permitting, project controls, finance, and owner communication collide. That makes the wedge easier to explain, easier to adopt, and easier to prove with pilot results.
